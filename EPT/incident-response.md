# Incident Response

This file contains notes on the incident response process and the steps taken when responding to cybersecurity incidents.

---

## What is Incident Response?

Incident Response (IR) is the process of identifying, managing, and recovering from cybersecurity incidents such as malware infections, unauthorized access, data breaches, and other security events.

The goal is to minimise damage, restore normal operations, and prevent similar incidents from occurring in the future.

---

## Why is Incident Response Important?

- Reduces the impact of security incidents
- Protects sensitive data
- Limits business disruption
- Helps organisations recover quickly
- Improves future security posture

---

## The Incident Response Lifecycle

### 1. Preparation

Preparation involves having the necessary tools, procedures, and training in place before an incident occurs.

Examples:
- Security policies
- Incident response plans
- Staff training
- Backups
- Monitoring tools

---

### 2. Identification

The goal is to determine whether a security incident has occurred.

Indicators may include:
- Unusual network traffic
- Malware alerts
- Failed login attempts
- Suspicious emails
- Unauthorized system changes

Example:
A workstation begins communicating with an unknown foreign IP address every five minutes.

---

### 3. Containment

Containment aims to prevent the incident from spreading and causing further damage.

Examples:
- Disconnecting affected devices from the network
- Isolating compromised systems
- Disabling affected accounts

Containment is often the first priority during an active incident.

---

### 4. Eradication

The root cause of the incident is identified and removed.

Examples:
- Removing malware
- Deleting malicious files
- Closing exploited vulnerabilities
- Removing unauthorized accounts

---

### 5. Recovery

Systems are restored to normal operation.

Examples:
- Restoring systems from backups
- Reconnecting systems to the network
- Monitoring for signs of reinfection

---

### 6. Lessons Learned

After the incident is resolved, the organisation reviews what happened and identifies ways to improve.

Examples:
- Updating security controls
- Improving user awareness training
- Updating incident response procedures
- Implementing additional monitoring

---

## Common Incident Response Tools

- Antivirus and EDR solutions
- SIEM platforms
- Firewall logs
- Network monitoring tools
- Forensic analysis tools

---

## What I Learned

- Containment is often the highest priority during an active incident.
- Incident response is a structured process rather than a single action.
- Investigations rely on evidence from logs, systems, and network activity.
- Lessons learned help strengthen security and prevent future incidents.
